## ![[safer_theory.avif]]
Label: safer theory — patchouli

Essential oils are extracted from plants through steam distillation. Patchouli requires a longer process — the leaves must be dried and aged before distillation.

Steam passes through the plant material, absorbing aromatic compounds, then cools and condenses back into water. The essential oil separates on the surface and is collected.

From around 500 kg of dried patchouli leaves, about 9 kg of essential oil is produced. Unlike many natural fragrance components, patchouli continues to develop over time, deepening its scent profile rather than fading.
